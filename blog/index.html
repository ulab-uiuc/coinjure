Introduction

Trading is rapidly entering an AI-native era. Although modern quantitative technologies have heavily automated lower-level trading mechanics—such as high-frequency execution, statistical arbitrage, and basic algorithmic rebalancing—a substantial portion of trading across diverse markets still relies heavily on human oversight. In equity markets, human traders still drive qualitative news analysis and narrative-based stock selection. In options markets, human intuition is required for managing complex volatility surfaces and tail risks. Furthermore, high-level operational decisions, such as multi-strategy management, capital allocation, and dynamic market selection, remain fundamentally human-driven. However, with the rapid advancement of LLM agents, these complex reasoning and management tasks can now be fully automated. To unlock this potential, we need purpose-built, agent-native trading systems that empower AI to execute these high-level functions, ultimately surpassing human traders in both speed and decision-making quality.

Why Prediction Markets?

While equity and options markets are dominated by entrenched institutional infrastructure and massive capital, prediction markets (such as Polymarket and Kalshi) offer an ideal, accessible testing ground for AI agents. More importantly, prediction markets are mostly semantic and event-driven. Prices fluctuate dramatically based on real-world news, political events, and social narratives. Since LLM-based agents natively excel at parsing massive amounts of qualitative text and extracting actionable sentiment, prediction markets allow them to fully leverage their semantic reasoning advantages to mine alpha.

Why an Agent-Native CLI?

Existing trading platforms are fundamentally constrained by their human-centric design. They rely heavily on visual dashboards and Graphical User Interfaces (GUIs) tailored to human cognitive limits. AI agents, however, do not perceive the market through screens; they require high-bandwidth, programmatic data streams and direct execution environments. By utilizing an agent-native Command Line Interface (CLI), the friction of UIs is entirely removed.

Why Agent Skills Are Not Enough?

A common misconception in AI development is treating “agent skills” as a silver bullet. In reality, skills are merely the instruction manuals for the tools provided to the agent; they are fundamentally complementary to the underlying trading system. If a system only provides raw, low-level APIs, the agent must generate extensive, complex code to execute even basic functions. This not only throttles execution efficiency but also inflates the context window with tedious operational logic, increasing the probability of errors and hallucinations.

The Coinjure Solution

To address these critical gaps, we propose Coinjure: an agent-native trading scaffold explicitly designed for prediction markets. Rather than patching AI onto legacy human systems, Coinjure is built from the ground up around the cognitive and operational needs of LLM agents, offering three core pillars:

  • End-to-End Strategy Discovery Pipeline: Coinjure provides a complete, natively integrated workflow tailored for semantic, event-driven environments. It equips agents with specialized modules covering the entire trading lifecycle: automated data ingestion, strategy discovery, backtesting, paper trading, and live execution.
  • Dual-Layer Control System: We invert traditional trading roles. AI agents operate via a streamlined, high-bandwidth Command Line Interface (CLI) to autonomously drive the system. Meanwhile, human traders transition to a pure supervisory role, utilizing a dedicated top-level monitor to observe, pause, resume, or safely terminate operations without interfering with the agent’s micro-mechanics.
  • Cognitive Scaffolding over Raw Skills: Instead of forcing agents to write complex code for deterministic tasks (e.g., order state polling, slippage calculation, or backtest rendering), Coinjure abstracts these into robust, high-level functions. This deep integration transforms basic “skills” into powerful capabilities, drastically reducing context-window bloat and freeing the LLM to focus entirely on semantic reasoning and alpha discovery.

1. Strategy Discovery

In Coinjure, strategy discovery focuses on identifying and exploiting the structural, statistical, or semantic relationships between different prediction markets. Rather than relying on isolated market signals, the system seeks out specific groupings—such as identical markets across platforms (same_event), mutually exclusive outcomes (complementary), or cointegrated price series (correlated). Once a distinct relationship is identified, Coinjure applies a specialized trading strategy tailored to extract value from that specific market dynamic.

2. Strategy Backtesting

Before any capital is risked, every discovered relationship undergoes rigorous validation through backtesting. The system replays historical market data, utilizing either Parquet order book snapshots or exchange price history APIs, directly through the trading engine. This simulation is powered by a PaperTrader module that ensures realistic order execution by accurately accounting for historical fill rates and actual order book liquidity.

3. Trade Execution

Strategies that successfully pass the backtesting phase are seamlessly transitioned through a designated paper-to-live trading pipeline. This entire execution process is managed by an asynchronous, event-driven TradingEngine that handles the complexities of live market interaction, order routing, and state management.

4. Human Monitoring

To ensure safety and visibility, Coinjure provides a comprehensive, fully decoupled real-time observability suite for human operators. A Textual-based TUI dashboard connects to the engine via Unix domain sockets, allowing operators to monitor portfolio values, order books, and strategy decisions, while also providing granular control to pause, resume, or hot-swap strategies on the fly. Additionally, the system features robust emergency protocols, including a global CLI kill-switch and instant TUI emergency stops, all supported by an isolated alerting framework that broadcasts critical events without risking the stability of the core trading engine.
